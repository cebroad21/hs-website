Healthcare professionals

Your role

You should signpost to the online application and promote the free vitamins. You may also help distribute the vitamins.

Uptake of the scheme is more likely if you take the time to explain its health benefits. Social care assessments are ideal opportunities to promote the scheme.

You can view uptake data for the voucher version of the scheme for:

  • England (Excel: 3.1MB)
  • Wales (Excel: 366KB)
  • Northern Ireland (Excel: 69KB)

You can also access promotional items from the online resource library (opens in a new tab).

Healthy Start vitamins

Healthy Start vitamins are important because:

  • 8% of children under 5 in the UK do not get enough vitamin A
  • families in lower-income groups tend to get less vitamin C
  • young children, teenagers and those from ethnic minorities are at risk of not getting enough vitamin D
  • when pregnant and breastfeeding, especially at a younger age, there's a higher risk of not getting enough vitamin D

Find out more about:

  • Healthy Start vitamins and who can get them (opens in a new tab)
  • using vitamins (opens in a new tab)

What the vitamins contain

The daily dose of 1 Healthy Start tablet contains:

  • 70 milligrams of vitamin C
  • 10 micrograms of vitamin D
  • 400 micrograms of folic acid

The daily dose of 5 Healthy Start children's drops contains:

  • 233 micrograms of vitamin A
  • 20 milligrams of vitamin C
  • 10 micrograms of vitamin D3

Distributing the vitamins for free in England and Wales

Beneficiaries will show their Healthy Start card to get their vitamins for free.

You can reclaim the cost using the return form PDF we email to you. On the form you need to:

  • tell us how many tablets and drops you have distributed each quarter
  • digitally sign it

If you cannot digitally sign the PDF:

  • email nhsbsa.hsvitaminreturns@nhs.net to request an Excel version
  • take a photo of your signature
  • add this as an image on the return form

You should submit your form each quarter. The deadlines for each year are:

  • 31 January
  • 30 April
  • 31 July
  • 31 October

Helping those excluded from claiming public funds

Those that cannot claim ‘public funds’ (opens in a new tab) (for example, benefits) may be eligible for Healthy Start. This would either be because:

  • of their immigration status
  • they do not have an immigration status

Their biometric residence permit (BRP) or their online immigration status (opens in a new tab) will say if they cannot claim public funds. They might also have a letter from the Home Office about it.

They should email healthystartclaim@dhsc.gov.uk to request an application form if:

  • they cannot claim public funds
  • they have at least 1 British child under 4 years old
  • their family earns £408 or less per month after tax
