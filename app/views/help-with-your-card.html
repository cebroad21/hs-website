Help with your card or vouchers

You'll receive your card by post. It'll come with your first payment on it and instrustions on how to activate it

Money will be automatically added to your card every 4 weeks.

How to get help

You can call 0300 330 2090 to:

  • activate your card
  • get your PIN
  • check your balance
  • report your card as lost, damaged or stolen

This phone line is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Activate your card and find out your PIN before you use your card for the first time

You'll need your:

  • Card
  • date of birth
  • client ID (located at the top of the letter that comes with your card

If you did not give us an email address or a phone number when you apply, you will get your PIN in the post. Your PIN will be sent out 2 days after your card is sent to you

Get a reminder of your PIN or check your balance

You’ll need your

  • Card
  • date of birth
  • 6-digit telephone access code

You'll get your telephone access code when you activate your card.

You can also check your balance at a cashpoint using your PIN

Report your card as lost, damaged or stolen

You'll need your:

  • Card
  • client ID (located at the top of the letter that comes with your card

Any transactions made before the card is reported lost or stolen will be charged to your account

Turning off contactless payments

If you do not want your Healthy Start card to be contactless, email healthy.start@nhsbsa.nhs.uk or call 0300 330 7010. You will only be able to use your card with your PIN.

Using vouchers

You’ll receive your Healthy Start vouchers by post every 4 weeks.

You’ll receive the vouchers for Healthy Start foods and Healthy Start vitamins together.

You’ll get Healthy Start food vouchers every 4 weeks, but receive a valid vitamins voucher every 8 weeks.

If your Healthy Start vitamin voucher has “VOID” printed on it, you’re not due any Healthy Start vitamins until the next month and you cannot use the vitamin voucher.

Where you can use your vouchers

You can use your Healthy Start vouchers in any shop that is registered to take part in the scheme (Excel: 335KB). If a retailer is not on this list, please ask the retailer if they can accept vouchers directly.

Other types of shops that may accept Healthy Start vouchers include:

  • corner shops
  • food co-ops
  • greengrocers
  • market stalls
  • milk floats or vans
  • pharmacies
  • supermarkets
